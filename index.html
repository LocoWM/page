LWM: Locomotion World Model for Quadrupedal Robots

A brief introduction to LWM, along with the presentation of videos showcasing simulation experiments and physical experiments.

Abstract

Designing a generalized policy to enable distinct quadrupedal robots to learn versatile locomotion skills stands as a challenging task. While existing works have proposed a range of approaches, it remains an open problem to achieve both skill generalization and platform generalization simultaneously. To address this gap, we develop a Locomotion World Model (LWM) that leverages a single policy to enable multi-skill acquisition and cross-platform deployment on real-world quadrupedal hardware. To cover morphology variations, we first collect a generalized robotic trajectory dataset exclusively in simulation. Then, we pre-train a motion compressor, which effectively captures the structured motion representation of various locomotion skills. Finally, the extracted representation is incorporated as the LWM’s observation, and a key morphology randomization mechanism is introduced during training to enhance policy generalizability across different platforms and tasks. When deployed in real-world settings, the proposed LWM achieves zero-shot skill deployment on three quadrupedal robots with distinct hardware architectures, validating the effectiveness of our approach in both skill generalization and platform generalization.
